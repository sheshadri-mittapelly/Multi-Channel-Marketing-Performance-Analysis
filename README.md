# 📊 Marketing Performance & Pricing Strategy Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes marketing channel performance using key business metrics such as Revenue, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS), and Campaign Distribution.

The objective is to identify which marketing channels generate the highest return and how budget allocation impacts profitability.

This project demonstrates practical data analytics skills used in real-world business decision making.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Companies invest in multiple marketing channels but often do not know:

• which channel generates highest revenue  
• which channel has lowest acquisition cost  
• which campaign provides best return on investment  
• where marketing budget should be optimized  

This project answers those questions using data analysis.

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### 1. Revenue
Total income generated from marketing campaigns.

Helps measure effectiveness of each marketing channel.

---

### 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
Average cost required to acquire one customer.

Formula:

CAC = Total Marketing Cost / Total Customers Acquired

Lower CAC indicates better efficiency.

---

### 3. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
Measures revenue generated per unit of advertising spend.

Formula:

ROAS = Revenue / Advertising Cost

Higher ROAS indicates more profitable campaigns.

---

### 4. Campaign Distribution
Shows how marketing campaigns are distributed across channels.

Helps identify overuse or underuse of specific channels.

## 🔍 Key Insights

• some channels generate high revenue but also high cost  
• best channel is not always the one with most campaigns  
• high ROAS channels provide better profitability  
• balancing budget across channels improves performance  

Example:

a channel may have high revenue but low ROAS, meaning high spending.

another channel may generate lower revenue but very high ROAS, meaning efficient marketing.
